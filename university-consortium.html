The Purpose and the Objectives of the Consortium

The purpose of establishing the Consortium is to work with partner universities to raise the level of scientific research and teaching, in order to recognize nationally and internationally their prestige and their classification as reference universities in Europe, to initiate the improvement of the legislative framework for higher education and university research, as well as the unitary definition of university study programs and the minimum standards for them, along with the degrees and qualifications acquired after their graduation.
The partnered universities cooperate in order to achieve the following objectives of the Consortium:

  • ensure the efficient use of the Consortium’s material, financial and human resources, as well as its own assets;

  • propose the principles of cooperation and common strategic objectives for all the institutions in the Consortium;

  • promote academic programs, respectively joint scientific research projects;

  • support measures to strengthen and expand: scientific research, its own internationally published publications and adherence to the category of leading European universities;

  • identify areas of excellence in research, in order to provide logistical and financial support to research centers and projects resulting from the pooling of resources of the institutions involved;

  • develop an integrated admission strategy and a unique student guide at the Consortium level, listing all the courses and specializations they can take, based on the accumulation and transfer of study credits to any of the partner universities;

  • create a common set of guidelines to take and pass undergraduate exams, master’s dissertations, and doctoral theses

  • facilitate the mobility of teachers and students within the Consortium;

  • promote proposals and solutions in order to improve the legislation and regulations of higher education in the country, to increase the competitiveness of Romanian universities and their social relevance;

  • support the improvement and efficiency of the legislative and logistical framework for Lifelong Learning;

  • initiate proposals for the unitary establishment of the fields and specializations of undergraduate, master’s and doctoral university education;

  • initiate proposals for the establishment of minimum standards for the authorization and accreditation of study programs;

  • initiate proposals for the designation of qualifications and titles acquired after graduation;

  • coordinate joint efforts in the search for the establishment of doctoral studies in Romania, with the goal of having a significant impact on research and boosting the competitiveness and prestige of Romanian universities;

  • direct the name and content of the master’s programs, to ensure the convergence of the procedures used by the partner universities in authorizing these programs;

  • promote the diversification, evaluation and ranking of Romanian universities, with differentiated funding, in order to improve the system of national recognition of contributions and scientific values of higher education institutions, corresponding to the competitive scope of each of them;

  • make some joint investments in the interest and for the use of all partner universities

  • provide scholarships and other forms of material support to students and other members of the academic community within the Consortium’s universities;

  • sponsor actions and programs initiated by the partners of the Consortium, as well as in favor of scientific, cultural, humanitarian or religious organizations;

  • any other objectives that are consistent with the purpose of the Consortium mentioned above

The iTransfer mission is to stimulate innovation and technology transfer in order to capitalize on research results by introducing them into the economic circuit in the form of innovative products and services.
